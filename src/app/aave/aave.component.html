Calculation description

1. Daily Returns

For each asset in the portfolio, we compute daily returns from historical closing prices over the last 365 days:

rt = (Pt - Pt-1) / Pt-1

Where Pt is the closing price at day t.

2. EWMA Volatility (Exponentially Weighted Moving Average)

Traditional volatility treats all historical days equally. EWMA gives more weight to recent days, making it more reactive to current market conditions. We use the RiskMetrics standard with λ = 0.94:

σt2 = λ · σt-12 + (1 - λ) · rt2
σEWMA = √σt2

Where:

  • λ = 0.94 — decay factor (94% of the previous variance is carried forward)
  • rt — daily return at day t
  • The initial seed σ02 is the full-sample variance

If a crash happened last week, EWMA volatility will be significantly higher than a simple standard deviation that averages it over the whole year.

3. Stressed Correlation

Standard correlations are computed over all market conditions, including calm periods. However, during market crashes, correlations between crypto assets spike toward 1 — assets that seemed uncorrelated suddenly drop together.

To capture this, we compute correlation only on the worst 10% of trading days for the target asset:

ρstressed(A, B) = Pearson(rAstress, rBstress)

Where rstress contains only the returns from the worst 10% of days (sorted by most negative returns of asset A).

The Pearson correlation formula:

ρ(A, B) = ∑(rA,i - r̄A)(rB,i - r̄B) / √[∑(rA,i - r̄A)2 · ∑(rB,i - r̄B)2]

This gives a conservative, crisis-period estimate of how assets move together — exactly when it matters most for liquidation risk.

4. Systemic Liquidation Price (per asset)

AAVE triggers liquidation when the Health Factor drops below a target (default = 1):

HF = ∑(Qi · Pi · LTi) / D

Where:

  • Qi — quantity of asset i
  • Pi — current price of asset i
  • LTi — liquidation threshold of asset i (set by AAVE governance)
  • D — total debt

The systemic liquidation price answers: "At what price of asset A would the entire portfolio reach the target Health Factor, assuming all other assets co-move according to their Beta?"

Step A — Total Current Collateral
Ctotal = ∑ Qi · Pi · LTi
Step B — Beta-Weighted Collateral

For each target asset A, we compute how much collateral would move if A drops:

βB→A = ρstressed(A, B) · σEWMA,B / σEWMA,A

Beta answers: "If asset A drops by 1%, asset B drops by β%"

CbetaA = ∑ Ci · βi→A

Where Ci = Qi · Pi · LTi and βA→A = 1 (an asset is perfectly correlated with itself).

Step C — Liquidation Price
Droprequired = (Ctotal - D · HFtarget) / CbetaA
PliquidationA = PA · (1 - Droprequired)

This is the price at which asset A would need to fall — accounting for all other portfolio assets falling proportionally via Beta — for AAVE to trigger liquidation on your position.

5. Portfolio Value at Risk (VaR)

VaR estimates the maximum expected loss in a single day at a given confidence level. We use the parametric (variance-covariance) method with EWMA volatilities and stressed correlations.

Portfolio Volatility
σp2 = ∑i ∑j wi · wj · σEWMA,i · σEWMA,j · ρstressed(i, j)

Where wi = Vi / Vtotal is the weight of asset i in the portfolio by USD value.

VaR Calculation
VaR95% = Vtotal × 1.645 × σp
VaR99% = Vtotal × 2.326 × σp

Where:

  • Vtotal — total collateral value in USD
  • 1.645 and 2.326 — z-scores from the standard normal distribution

VaR 95%: On 95% of days, your portfolio loss will not exceed this amount. Only 1 day out of 20 could be worse.

VaR 99%: On 99% of days, your portfolio loss will not exceed this amount. Only 1 day out of 100 could be worse.
